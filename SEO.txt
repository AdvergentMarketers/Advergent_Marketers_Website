Phase 0: The Target Vector (Before On-Page)
Before touching any code, you need a mathematical target. If you optimize blindly, you rank for nothing.

Keyword Mapping: Assign exactly 1 primary keyword and 2-3 secondary keywords to each existing page.

Example: The Home page targets "Digital Growth Agency" and "Full-Stack Marketing Architecture". The About page targets "Fractional CMO India" or "Technical Marketing Experts".

Search Intent Matching: Ensure your copy actually answers what the user is looking for when they type those words.

Phase 1: Technical & On-Page Optimization (Your Step 1)
Now you apply those targets to your Next.js infrastructure.

Dynamic Metadata: In your layout.tsx and page.tsx files, deploy the Next.js Metadata API. Write highly optimized <title> and <meta name="description"> tags using your target keywords.

Open Graph (OG) Images: Ensure every page has an og:image so when your links are shared on LinkedIn, Twitter, or iMessage, they generate a beautiful, matte-black preview card.

Schema Markup: Inject LocalBusiness and Organization JSON-LD into your root layout so Google's bots instantly understand who you are.

Phase 2: The Tracking Infrastructure (The "In-Between")
Before you launch a blog and drive traffic, you need the sensors in place to measure it.

Google Search Console (GSC): This is mandatory. Verify your domain with Google. This is how you force Google to index your site and how you see exactly what keywords are bringing you traffic.

Automated Sitemap: Install a package like next-sitemap to dynamically generate a sitemap.xml and robots.txt file every time you build the site. Submit this XML file to Google Search Console.

Analytics: Deploy Google Analytics 4 (GA4) or a privacy-first alternative (like Fathom or Plausible) to track user behavior, bounce rates, and lead conversions.

Phase 3: The Local Authority Node (The "In-Between")
Google Business Profile (GBP): Remember that perfectly optimized 750-character GBP description we wrote earlier? Deploy it now. Getting your agency verified on Google Maps establishes an immediate layer of trust and captures high-intent local/national search traffic while your global organic rankings build up.

Phase 4: The Content Ecosystem (Your Step 2)
Now you build the "Blog", but as an agency, do not call it a "Blog". Call it "Insights," "Protocols," or "Case Studies." It sounds much more premium.

MDX or Headless CMS: Set up Sanity, Strapi, or just local MDX files to manage your articles so you don't have to hardcode every new post.

Pillar Content Strategy: Write 3-4 massive, definitive guides (e.g., "The Ultimate Guide to E-Commerce Infrastructure in 2026"). Then, write smaller articles that link back to those main pillars.

Phase 5: Off-Page Authority (What's Next)
Once the content is live, you must prove to Google that the industry respects you.

High-Fidelity Backlinks: Get other reputable tech, business, and marketing websites to link to your Insights or Home page.

Digital PR: Get yourself quoted as an expert in industry publications.
